The Problem With Current Advice

So What Are Your Options?

Let's agree on something: the problem is real. The passive approach — keep your head down, do good work, hope someone notices — is no longer a strategy. It's a risk. So what can you actually do about it?

Option 1

Learn to Prompt AI

The prevailing wisdom says if you can master prompting — if you can talk to a chatbot better than everyone else — you'll be indispensable. Build your career on knowing the right prompt engineering techniques.

Verdict: Race to the Bottom
Option 2

Go to Med School, Law School, or Trade School

The advice is analytically sound: get into licensed professions (doctors, lawyers, engineers) or skilled trades (electricians, plumbers). These fields have regulatory moats and physical presence requirements that AI can't touch.

Verdict: Analytically Sound, Practically Useless
Why Both of These Fail

"Learn to prompt AI" is a race to the bottom. Prompting ability becomes less valuable with every model improvement because the models get better at understanding imprecise instructions. Building a career on prompting skill is building on sand.

"Go into trades, medicine, or law" is analytically correct but practically impossible for most people who need help right now. A 42-year-old marketing manager cannot go to medical school. A laid-off project coordinator cannot spend three years getting a law degree. The advice that sounds smart at a dinner party helps nobody at their kitchen table at 2 AM wondering how they will pay their mortgage.

The Real Question

"What skills can I stack onto who I already am that make me harder to replace?"

Sales Skills

Not because everyone should become a salesperson. But because the underlying skills of professional selling — building trust, diagnosing problems, navigating complex decisions, reading people, holding accountability — are the exact capabilities that sit squarely in the "hardest to replace" category.

And unlike becoming a doctor, lawyer, or licensed engineer, learning to sell does not require years of schooling, a professional license, or starting your career over.

It is the most accessible, high-value skill that creates AI-resistant career durability.
